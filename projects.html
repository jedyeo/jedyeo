Autonomous Coin Collecting Robot

As part of the an electrical engineering design course, my team was tasked with designing a fully autonomous coin collecting robot, capable of traversing an area defined by an electromagnetic field and picking up coins with the aid of a robotic arm and metal detector. We decided to implement additional features such as signal lighting and wireless Bluetooth communication to store and report robot data and activity.

Temperature Sensor

Written in Assembly language, the purpose of the project was to read voltages from an analog-to-digital converter, then plot temperature readings in Python through an SPI port. I added extra features such as the ability to swtich between plotting Celsius and Kelvin, a temperature alarm using interrupts, and an LCD interface.

Color Organ

As part of UBC's Concrete Toboggan's Electrical Sub-Team, I helped design and implement a color organ to complement the toboggan at Tech-X, the competition showcase. The circuit takes in input frequencies and outputs varying signals to RGB LEDs to simulate an RGB equalizer. Definitely a challenging yet interesting project!

Autonomous Claw

For my first real engineering project, my team was tasked with designing and programming an autonomous claw to pick up various objects. The claw closed and opened through the means of a sensor and was programmed using an Arduino microcontroller. The project was not tough because of the constrained parameters, rather the amount of teamwork and collaboration to successfully execute the project. Our claw ended up picking up the most items in our year!
